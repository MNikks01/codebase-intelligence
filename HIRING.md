# Codebase Intelligence — HIRING

Hire behind revenue (portfolio principle — BUSINESS_STRATEGY.md §5).

## Stage 0 — Solo founder ($0)
Build the engine with AI coding agents against this spec (D-011). The hard IP (RAG-over-code) is the founder's to learn deeply — it's the moat and the resume.

## Stage 1 — First engineer (~$0–10K MRR)
Full-stack engineer (TS/Next/Node) to own product surface (web app, API, dashboards) so founder focuses on the retrieval engine + customers. Contractor-first OK.

## Stage 2 — AI engineer (~$10–25K MRR)
**The most important hire for this product.** Owns retrieval quality, evals, embeddings, rerank, graph RAG, and COGS. Profile: strong RAG/embeddings/evals, pragmatic (no model training — D-001), obsessed with accuracy + cost. First 90 days: own the eval harness + push faithfulness/recall.

## Stage 3 — DevRel + Founding sales (~$25–50K MRR)
DevRel: technical content (RAG-over-code authority), OSS stewardship, the public demo, docs. Founding sales: Team/Enterprise/API motion once inbound proves out.

## Stage 4 — Scale (>$50K MRR)
SRE (indexing scale, K8s, on-prem), more AI eng (languages, agents), Support/Success, Design.

## Principles
Small + senior; hire the bottleneck (engine quality → product surface → distribution). The AI engineer hire is non-negotiable early because accuracy is the product. Use contractors to trial roles. Remote-first, equity-generous.

## What NOT to do
Don't hire sales before the public demo + PLG prove the motion. Don't hire juniors to brute-force the engine — accuracy needs senior judgment.
