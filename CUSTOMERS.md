# Codebase Intelligence — CUSTOMERS

## Ideal Customer Profiles

### ICP-1 — Growing engineering team with a maturing codebase (PRIMARY)
- 10–100 engineers; codebase 1–3+ years old, accelerating in size/complexity.
- Pain: onboarding slow, tribal knowledge, fear of refactoring, AI tools don't understand the code.
- Buys: faster onboarding, safer changes, trusted AI answers. Buyer: eng lead/staff/CTO; user: every dev.

### ICP-2 — Software agency / consultancy
- Constantly onboarding to *unfamiliar client* codebases.
- Pain: ramp time on each new engagement is pure cost.
- Buys: instant understanding of any codebase → higher utilization, faster delivery. High WTP.

### ICP-3 — Enterprise with legacy/large codebases (EXPANSION)
- 100–1000+ engineers; legacy systems few people understand; compliance needs.
- Pain: key-person risk, undocumented systems, risky changes, audit.
- Buys: institutional understanding, impact analysis, governance. Needs SSO/SOC2/on-prem. Large contracts, long cycle.

### ICP-4 — AI tool / agent builders (API/MCP customers)
- Building agents/products that need to understand code.
- Pain: building good RAG-over-code is hard; they'd rather buy the engine.
- Buys: our API/MCP server as infrastructure. Strategic (we power others).

### ICP-5 — Individual senior dev / OSS maintainer (FREE→PRO)
- Pain: understanding large/unfamiliar repos quickly.
- Adoption/evangelism engine; cheap Pro.

## Anti-personas
- Tiny/simple codebases (little to understand).
- Teams not yet feeling onboarding/scale pain.
- Non-technical users.

## Jobs To Be Done
1. "When a new dev joins, **make the codebase explain itself** so they ramp in days."
2. "When I face unfamiliar code, **answer my questions with citations** so I trust it."
3. "Before I change code, **tell me what it affects** so I don't break prod."
4. "Keep our docs and architecture maps **current automatically**."
5. "Give my agent **an API to understand our code**."

## Buyer vs. user
- **User:** individual devs (bottom-up, free tier, evangelize).
- **Buyer:** eng lead/CTO (Team), VP Eng/Security (Enterprise), or product teams (API).
- **Motion:** PLG land (free repo index) → Team (collab/governance) → Enterprise (security/scale) / API (usage).

## Prioritization
| Segment | When | Why |
|---------|------|-----|
| Senior devs / teams (free→Team) | Launch | Fast value, evangelism |
| Agencies | Launch→Y1 | High WTP, clear ROI |
| API/agent builders | Y1 | Strategic distribution |
| Enterprise | Y2 | Big contracts; needs SOC2/SSO/on-prem |

## Validation questions
- How long does onboarding take and what does it cost you?
- When you face unfamiliar code, how do you currently get answers?
- Have you tried "chat with codebase" tools? Why did/didn't they stick?
- Would you connect this repo? What would make you trust it with your code?
